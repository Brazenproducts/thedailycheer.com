Fluffy Local Wool Could Be the Hero Our Peatlands Need

May 15, 2026 · BBC Science

Here's a feel-good story straight from the rolling green hills of Northern Ireland — scientists have discovered that locally-sourced sheep's wool might be the secret ingredient to restoring precious peatlands. Who knew your favourite cosy jumper material had such superpowers?

Peatlands are incredible natural spaces that store huge amounts of carbon, helping keep our planet healthy. Restoring them has traditionally been costly and complicated, but researchers are now exploring how wool — a completely natural, renewable resource already abundant across Northern Ireland — can help rebuild these vital ecosystems without a hefty carbon price tag.

What makes this so wonderfully exciting is that it turns a local, everyday material into a genuine force for good. Farmers, conservationists, and communities could all work hand in hand on something truly meaningful. It's the kind of homegrown, ingenious thinking that reminds us that sometimes the most brilliant solutions have been right under our noses — or on our sheep — all along!
