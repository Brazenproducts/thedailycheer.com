Zoos Are Competing in a Tree Kangaroo Crunch-a-Thon and It's the Best Thing on the Internet

May 16, 2026 · 3 min read

📹 Tree Kangaroo Crunch-a-thon 2026 — YouTube

Zoos across the United States are doing something wonderful this week: posting videos of their tree kangaroos absolutely demolishing crunchy vegetables on social media. It's called the International Tree Kangaroo Crunch-a-Thon, and it's officially the most heartwarming competition on the internet right now.

What is the Crunch-a-Thon?

Organized by AZA SAFE: Tree Kangaroo of Papua New Guinea (that's Saving Animals From Extinction), the Crunch-a-Thon is a social media competition ahead of World Tree Kangaroo Day on May 21. Zoos and conservation organizations that work with tree kangaroos post videos on Instagram and Facebook of their tree roos eating something crunchy — and the internet votes with likes and views.

The competition categories are: Most Likes, Most Views, and Judges' Choice. Winners are announced May 17.

What are tree kangaroos?

Photo: Matschie's tree kangaroo at the Bronx Zoo — Wikimedia Commons

Tree kangaroos are the world's only arboreal kangaroos — there are 14 known species in the Dendrolagus genus. They're herbivorous marsupials with bushy tails, long arms, and padded back feet built for gripping branches. They live in rainforests across parts of Australia, Indonesia, and Papua New Guinea.

The Golden-mantled tree kangaroo is among the world's most endangered mammals and only lives in a tiny area of Papua New Guinea — which is why conservation programs like this matter so much.

The approved snack menu

The organizers even provided official crunchy food recommendations for maximum ASMR satisfaction: bell peppers, celery, romaine hearts, snap peas, green beans, cucumbers, and zucchini — with the caveat that cucumbers and zucchini "might not have the best crunch." (Fair point.)

Who's competing?

Roger Williams Park Zoo entered with a video of their three Matschie's tree kangaroos — pink-nosed, brown and white, and absolutely going to town on vegetables. "Our Zoo is home to three Matschie's tree kangaroos — a species native to the cloud forests of Papua New Guinea," they wrote.

Multiple zoos across the country have posted entries, each one more adorable than the last. The closeup shots of little snouts crunching through bell peppers? Pure joy.

Why this matters

Beyond being the most wholesome content you'll see today, the Crunch-a-Thon raises awareness for endangered tree kangaroo species. These animals face habitat loss from logging and agriculture in Papua New Guinea and Indonesia. Every share and like puts eyes on their conservation story.

In the words of the organizers: "Let the crunching begin!" 🥕🦘
