Louisiana Islanders Find New Ground and Carry Their Community Forward

May 16, 2026 · wire

The tight-knit residents of Isle de Jean Charles, a small Louisiana community, have always known how to look out for one another. When rising waters made their beloved island home increasingly difficult to live on, families made the brave decision to relocate to safer, higher ground — a move that took real courage and love for their community.

The transition wasn't without its bumps, as any big move can be. Neighbors had to rediscover what it means to put down roots somewhere new, and some missed the deep sense of place that came with generations of island living. Their honest reflections are a gift to other communities facing similar decisions.

What shines through most is the resilience of these remarkable people. Their voices and experiences are now helping other communities plan smarter, more thoughtful relocations — making sure that when people move, they bring the heart of their community right along with them.
