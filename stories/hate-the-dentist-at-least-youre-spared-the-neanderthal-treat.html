Be Grateful for Modern Dentistry: Neanderthals Had It Rough

May 16, 2026 · wire

We get it – dental appointments aren't anyone's idea of fun. But here's a fun fact that'll make you feel a lot better about your next checkup: at least you're not a Neanderthal! Researchers studying a single tooth discovered in a Russian cave found something fascinating: ancient grooves made by stone tools. Yep, Neanderthals were apparently doing their own dental work with rocks!

These prehistoric folks were surprisingly resourceful, using whatever tools they had on hand to deal with tooth troubles. While we don't know exactly why they were working on their teeth – maybe it was pain relief, maybe curiosity – it shows they cared enough to try something about it. That takes guts!

So the next time you're sitting in that comfortable dental chair with a trained professional using proper instruments, take a moment to appreciate how far we've come. Modern dentistry might not be thrilling, but it beats the stone-tool alternative by a mile. We're living in the golden age of keeping our smiles healthy!
