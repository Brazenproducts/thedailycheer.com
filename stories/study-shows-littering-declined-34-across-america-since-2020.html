Americans Are Keeping Streets Cleaner and the Numbers Prove It

May 14, 2026 · Good News Network

Here's something that'll make your walk outside feel a little brighter: littering across the United States has dropped by a whopping 34% since 2020. That's not a small dip — that's a massive, measurable shift in the way people are treating the world around them.

Researchers tracking litter trends found that communities big and small are doing better at keeping their streets, parks, and public spaces tidy. Whether it's more awareness, better habits formed during quieter pandemic years, or just a growing sense of pride in shared spaces, something wonderful is clearly clicking.

The best part? This kind of change happens one person at a time — and millions of people choosing to do the right thing adds up to something truly beautiful. So next time you toss something in the trash instead of on the ground, know that you're part of something bigger. Keep it up, America!
