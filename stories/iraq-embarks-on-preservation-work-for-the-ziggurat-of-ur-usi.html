Iraq Is Lovingly Restoring a 5,000-Year-Old Ancient Wonder

May 14, 2026 · Good News Network

Something truly wonderful is happening in southern Iraq right now. Workers are carefully restoring the Ziggurat of Ur, one of the oldest and most breathtaking structures ever built by human hands. And here's the really beautiful part — they're using bricks made from the exact same materials as the originals, just like ancient builders did five millennia ago.

The Ziggurat of Ur is a massive stepped temple that has stood since roughly 2100 BCE. It's the kind of place that makes you stop and think about just how creative and capable people have always been. Preserving it means future generations will get to experience that same sense of wonder.

This careful, respectful restoration is a love letter to human history — proof that we genuinely care about protecting our shared past. When communities come together to honor where we've been, it gives us all a little extra hope for where we're headed.
