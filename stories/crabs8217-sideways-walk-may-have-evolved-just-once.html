Science Reveals the Surprising Origin of Crabs' Adorable Sideways Shuffle

May 15, 2026 · wire

Here's a fun one for your day: scientists in Japan have been studying crabs — specifically, why they walk sideways — and the findings are absolutely delightful. After looking at 50 different crab species, researchers believe that iconic sideways scuttle traces all the way back to a single common ancestor. One little crab, way back when, started something truly special!

What makes this even more exciting is that this quirky walking style may have actually helped crabs thrive and diversify into the amazing variety of species we see today. In other words, being a little different might have been their greatest superpower.

It's a wonderful reminder that the natural world is full of surprises waiting to be discovered. Whether you spot a crab at the beach this summer or just think about one from your couch, know that their funny little walk has one very cool origin story behind it.
