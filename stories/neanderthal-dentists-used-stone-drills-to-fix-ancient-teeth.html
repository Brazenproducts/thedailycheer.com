Ancient Neanderthals Were Surprisingly Caring About Each Other's Teeth

May 16, 2026 · wire

Here's a fun reason to smile at your next dentist appointment! Researchers have made a fascinating discovery about our ancient Neanderthal cousins — turns out, they were taking care of each other's teeth long before modern dentistry was even a dream.

Scientists studying a tooth found in a Russian cave noticed tiny grooves inside it that match marks made by small stone tools. That means someone — a prehistoric helper, essentially — was doing their best to ease a neighbor's tooth troubles using whatever they had available. How thoughtful is that?

This discovery is a beautiful little window into just how human Neanderthals really were. They felt pain, they looked out for one another, and they tried to help. So next time you're reclining in that comfy dentist's chair, take a moment to feel grateful — and maybe marvel at how far we've all come together!
