Millions of Americans Are Connected to Maryland's Fascinating Colonial Roots

May 15, 2026 · wire

Here's a fun piece of history that might just give you goosebumps: researchers have discovered that a whopping 1.3 million people share DNA with the very first colonists who settled in Maryland centuries ago. That's a whole lot of family history hiding in plain sight!

Scientists dug deep into genealogical records and genetic data to trace living descendants back to those early Maryland settlers. It's the kind of painstaking, loving work that turns dusty old documents into real, breathing family stories — and the results are absolutely remarkable.

Just think about it: somewhere out there, more than a million people are walking around carrying a tiny genetic thread that connects them to pioneers who built something new from the ground up. Whether you know it or not, you could be one of them. History isn't just in textbooks — sometimes it's written right inside of us.
